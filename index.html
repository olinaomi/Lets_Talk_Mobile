How much is healthy?

Set household guidelines like screen free areas to get them into a routine of putting down their device. Don’t trade all print media for screens. Not all books and boardgames should be replaced by onscreen equivalents. Most importantly you should model good behaviour. You are your child’s first teacher!

Trolling, also know as cyberbullying, is a form online harassment.

A troll is someone who makes unsolicited or controversial comments with the intent to start
an argument online. The worst trolling happens
on YouTube and social networks such as Instagram, Tumblr and Twitter and is most common in children between the ages of 12 to 16 years.
How to deal with online trolls?

Trolls try to provoke a reaction so you should encourage your child to ignore them. Cyberbullying should be taken more seriously and should be reported to a parent or adult they feel close to. Children should be reminded that if they see something upsetting online they can block or
report that user.
